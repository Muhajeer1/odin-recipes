Hychyn

Hychyn (or khychin) is a traditional Caucasian flatbread stuffed with various filings like potatoes, cheese, or ground meat. To make it, prepare a soft dough using flour, kefir (or yogurt), salt, and baking soda. Let it rest for 20–30 minutes. Meanwhile, make your filing — for example, mashed potatoes mixed with crumbled cheese (like suluguni or feta) or sautéed ground beef with onions and spices.

Divide the dough into balls, roll each one out, place a spoonful of filing in the center, and seal it like a pouch. Gently flatten into a thick disc, then cook each hychyn on a dry hot skillet until golden on both sides. Brush with butter and serve warm — perfect with sour cream or tea.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup kefir (or plain yogurt)
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda and salt
  • 2–3 tbsp vegetable oil or melted butter (optional, for softer dough)
  • 3–li medium potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 150–200g cheese (suluguni, feta, or mozzarella)

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix:3 cups flour, 1/2 tsp salt,1/2 tsp baking soda
  2. Add 1 cup kefir (or plain yogurt) and mix until a soft dough forms.
  3. Knead briefly, cover with a towel, and let it rest for 20–30 minutes.
  4. Boil and mash 3–li potatoes.
  5. Mix in 150–200g crumbled or grated cheese.
  6. Add a pinch of salt (taste it — cheese might already be salty).
